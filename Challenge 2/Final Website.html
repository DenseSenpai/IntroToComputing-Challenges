The History of Indie Games

The history of indie games is a fascinating journey that traces its roots to the early days of the video game industry. In the 1970s and 1980s, when the industry was in its infancy, games were often developed by small teams or even individuals due to the limited resources and technology available. These early pioneers laid the groundwork for what would later become the indie game movement. As technology advanced, larger game development studios became the norm, dominating the industry with blockbuster titles. However, in the late 20th century and early 21st century, the rise of digital distribution platforms and the democratization of game development tools played a pivotal role in the resurgence of indie games. Developers could now create and distribute their games without the need for significant financial backing or traditional publishing deals. The indie game movement gained momentum as developers embraced the opportunity to explore innovative ideas, experiment with unique gameplay mechanics, and tell unconventional stories.

Top 10 Must-Play Indie Games

Discover hidden gems in the gaming world with our list of the top 10 must-play indie games. These unique and creative titles offer a fresh gaming experience.

  1. SEA OF STARS

    -is a 2023 role-playing video game by Sabotage Studio. The game was released for Windows, Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X/S and is set thousands of years before Sabotage Studio's previous game The Messenger (2018).

  2. Cocoon

    -is a puzzle game released in 2023, developed by Geometric Interactive and published by Annapurna Interactive. In the game, players take on the role of a beetle that can traverse between different worlds, embarking on a journey to solve puzzles that unveil the mysteries of the universe.

  3. Venba

    -The narrative unfolds as Venba and her husband embark on a journey from Tamil Nadu, a southern Indian state, to start afresh in Toronto, Canada. The couple grapples with the challenges posed by a new language, unfamiliar culture, and a scarcity of job opportunities. Despite the hardships, they hold onto hope, especially for their newborn son, envisioning a smoother experience for him. As Kavin matures, Venba endeavors to impart a deep appreciation for Tamil language and culture. However, she encounters obstacles as Kavin swiftly adapts to the Canadian way of life.

  4. A Space for the Unbound

    -was released on January 19, 2023, for Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Windows, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X/S. The game is set in the late 1990s and follows Atma and his girlfriend Raya, who live in a suburban area in Indonesia, as they explore their newly attained magical abilities and deal with supernatural powers that threaten their existence.

  5. Viewfinder

    -started off as an experiment by indie game developer Matt Stark around November 2019. Stark posted a video to social media showing the ability to dynamically take a photograph in a game world and then place that photo to overwrite the world with the contents of the photo.

  6. Dredge

    -started development in 2020 by New Zealand studio Black Salt Games. The team was inspired by other games with oppressive atmospheres, like Papers, Please and Frostpunk. Fog was used as an artistic tool to contrast the day, which is almost completely clear, to the night, where the player can barely see in front of themselves. A developer described the process of creating the game's mutant fish, saying they were "coming up with the craziest adjectives to describe these messed up fish"

  7. Jusant

    -is a puzzle platforming game by Don't Nod. The player controls a lone wanderer as they climb a desolate tower, filled with the artifacts of civilizations long past. It was released in October 2023 for Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X and Series S.

  8. Undying

    -is a 2023 survival video game developed by Vanimals and published by Skystone Games. Set in a zombie apocalypse, players control a woman who has been bitten by a zombie and must teach her child to survive before she loses her humanity.

  9. Cassette Beasts

    -is inspired by the Pokémon video game series and follows the progress of the player as they transform into monsters using a cassette player while trying to return to their home. Cassette Beasts received generally favorable reviews from critics, who commented positively on its complexity in comparison to Pokémon.

  10. Videoverse

    -is a 2023 narrative adventure game developed and published by Kinmoku. Players control a teenage gamer in 2003 and help him navigate relationships on a fictional social network dedicated to his favorite video game.
